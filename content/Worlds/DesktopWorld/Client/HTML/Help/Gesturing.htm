Gesturing

In Desktop World, gestures are motions displayed in the 3-D view that your character can perform. You can gesture to other members by clicking a gesture button or by typing a gesture keyword in the chat text area and pressing ENTER. Gestures are represented by the small buttons above the chat history area.

Clicking a gesture button makes your character perform a gesture in the 3-D view. While your character is gesturing, the chat history area shows that you performed the gesture. For example, if your character is named John, clicking Wave causes both a gesture to be performed and a statement to appear in the chat history area that says, "John waves."

You can also type certain keywords in the chat text box to perform gestures. Gesture keywords are recognized wherever they appear in a statement; however, only the first keyword in the statement will cause a gesture to be performed. For example, if you type, "Well hello smile," in the chat text area and press ENTER, only the Wave gesture associated with the keyword "hello" is performed, because it is the first keyword in the statement. The keyword "smile" has no effect, because` it is not the first keyword in the statement.

If your camera view is set to facing you, you can see your gestures in the 3-D area. If your camera view is set to over-the-shoulder, you can see every gesture except Smile and Turn Around.

Note If you place a member on your My Ignored list, his or her gestures will not be recorded in your chat history view. The same applies to your gestures if another guest has added you to his or her ignored list.



Buttons and keywords for gestures
Button Keyword Your Action
wave ...waves
hello ...waves
turn around ...turns around
smile ...smiles
:) ...smiles
flirt ...flirts
shrug ...shrugs
? ...shrugs
N/A sad ...frowns
N/A :( ...frowns


To perform a gesture
Choose one of these options:
  • Click a gesture button, such as Wave .
  • Type one of the gesture keywords in the chat text box and press ENTER.
